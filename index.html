Hi! I am Srinivas Gowriraj, a first-year ML/NLP graduate student (MS in Intelligent Information Systems) at the Language Technologies Institute at Carnegie Mellon University - School of Computer Science. I am advised by Prof. Graham Neubig, where I work on low-resource multilingual NLP.

Before joining CMU, I worked as a Software Engineer at ServiceNow, where I spearheaded research projects on machine learning, deep learning and NLP for the ITSM business unit. I applied my research to develop the multilingual virtual agent and Natural Language Understanding (NLU) model for ITSM customers.

I graduated with a B.E in Information Technology from Chaitanya Bharathi Institute of Technology , India 2020. During my undergrad, I spent my final year doing research with Prof. Trupthi Mukund on classroom lecture video summarization. Also, I worked as a Machine Learning Intern at Caravel.AI , where I created content on various machine learning algorithms for the KEATS online learning application.

Research Interests

I am passionate about research involving the theoretical as well as the applied study of deep learning and natural language processing. My overarching research aim is to design a framework to identify the optimal set of source languages and the number of data points from each source language such that upon finetuning the multilingual model on selected data points the performance on selected target low-resource language should be maximized. I am addressing this research problem in a fully computational manner i.e without using any heuristics about languages and language families. I am leveraging the concepts of active learning, meta-learning and reinforcement learning to solve the research problem.
